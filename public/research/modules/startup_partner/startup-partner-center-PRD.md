## Executive Summary

The Startup Partner Center is a strategic addition to the Allkons M construction materials marketplace, designed to create a structured affiliate partner ecosystem. This platform enables qualified partners to source products from eligible shops, create quotations, obtain shop approvals, and deliver approved quotations to buyers. The system supports multi-shop quoting, dual payment modes, and comprehensive team management while preparing for future evolution into Affiliate Community Marketing (ACM).

---

## 1. Problem Statement

### 1.1 Current Market Problem
Construction materials procurement in Thailand suffers from fragmentation and inefficiency:
- **Contractors and buyers** struggle to find reliable suppliers and compare prices across multiple shops
- **Suppliers** lack effective channels to reach qualified buyers and expand market reach
- **Price discovery** is opaque and time-consuming, requiring multiple contacts and negotiations
- **Trust and verification** are significant barriers in B2B construction transactions

### 1.2 Specific Pain Points
- **Partners** cannot efficiently source and quote from multiple shops simultaneously
- **Shops** lack controlled channels to extend their sales reach through qualified affiliates
- **Buyers** receive inconsistent quotations with varying quality and reliability
- **Platform** misses revenue opportunities from commission-based affiliate sales

### 1.3 Market Opportunity
The Thai construction materials market represents a significant opportunity for a structured affiliate program:
- Estimated market size: THB 500+ billion annually
- Growing demand for digital procurement solutions
- Need for trusted intermediary services in construction supply chain

- **Goals**: Ensure program quality, manage approvals, maintain platform integrity

## 4. User Journeys & Workflows

### 4.1 Partner Onboarding Journey
1. **Discovery**: Learn about partner program through Allkons M platform
2. **Application**: Complete registration with territory and shop selections
3. **Verification**: undergo KYC/KYB verification process
4. **Approval**: Receive dual approval from Allkons Admin and selected shops
5. **Activation**: Gain access to partner workspace and sourcing tools
6. **Training**: Complete onboarding and learn platform usage

### 4.2 Quotation Creation Journey
1. **Requirement Capture**: Receive buyer requirements (Line, call, offline)
2. **Product Sourcing**: Search eligible shops within territory for required products
3. **Comparison**: Compare prices, promotions, and availability across shops
4. **Quotation Draft**: Create draft quotation with selected products and shops
5. **Shop Approval**: Submit for shop approval (30-day SLA)
6. **Buyer Delivery**: Share approved quotation with buyer
7. **Order Tracking**: Monitor order progress and commission status

### 4.3 Buyer Purchase Journey
1. **Quotation Receipt**: Receive quotation link from partner
2. **Review**: Compare options, view shop branding, evaluate terms
3. **Payment**: Choose platform payment or direct payment with slip upload
4. **Order Confirmation**: Receive order confirmation and tracking information
5. **Delivery Tracking**: Monitor order progress through delivery
6. **Completion**: Confirm receipt and trigger commission calculation

## 7. Functional Requirements

### 7.1 Authentication & Partner Management

#### FR-001: MUST - Partner Registration
**Description**: Users must be able to register as Startup Partners using existing Allkons accounts
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- User can initiate partner application from existing Allkons account
- System validates KYC status before allowing registration
- User can specify sales territory (Province/District)
- User can select target shops from eligible options
- System validates territory-shop compatibility
- Application triggers dual approval workflow

#### FR-002: MUST - Dual Approval Workflow
**Description**: Partner applications must require approval from both Allkons Admin and Shop owners
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Application submitted to Allkons Admin for initial approval
- Shop owners receive requests for relationship approval
- Both approvals must be completed for activation
- System tracks approval status and timestamps
- Rejection reasons are captured and communicated
- Approval history is maintained for audit purposes

#### FR-003: MUST - Territory Management
**Description**: Partners must be assigned specific territories with shop compatibility validation
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Partners can specify Province/District territories
- System validates shop service coverage against territories
- Territory changes require re-approval
- Territory boundaries are clearly defined and enforced
- Partners can view available shops within their territory

### 7.2 Product Sourcing & Discovery

#### FR-004: MUST - Product Search
**Description**: Partners must be able to search products from affiliate-eligible shops within their territory
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Search returns products only from approved shop relationships
- Search results are filtered by territory coverage
- Search supports product name, Master SKU, and category filters
- Search results include real-time pricing and availability
- Search performance is under 2 seconds for typical queries

#### FR-005: MUST - Multi-Shop Comparison
**Description**: Partners must be able to compare products across multiple approved shops
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Comparison view shows same products from different shops
- Price differences are clearly highlighted
- Shop promotions and discounts are displayed
- Availability status is shown for each shop
- Partners can select products from multiple shops for single quotation

#### FR-006: SHOULD - Price Visibility
**Description**: Partners must be able to view shop-specific pricing and promotions
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Shop pricing is displayed based on partner relationship
- Promotional pricing is clearly indicated
- Bulk pricing options are shown when available
- Price history is available for reference
- Currency and tax information is clearly displayed

### 7.3 Quotation Management

#### FR-007: MUST - Draft Quotation Creation
**Description**: Partners must be able to create draft quotations with products from multiple shops
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Partners can create quotations with products from multiple shops
- System automatically splits quotations by shop when necessary
- Draft quotations can be saved and edited before submission
- Quotation templates can be created for recurring needs
- System validates product availability before quotation creation

#### FR-008: MUST - Shop Approval Workflow
**Description**: Quotations must be approved by shops before being sent to buyers
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Quotations are submitted to relevant shops for approval
- 30-day SLA is enforced for shop responses
- Shops can approve, reject, or request modifications
- Approval status is tracked and communicated to partners
- Expired quotations are automatically handled

#### FR-009: MUST - Quotation Lifecycle Management
**Description**: Partners must be able to manage quotation status throughout the lifecycle
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Quotation statuses: Draft, Submitted, Approved, Rejected, Expired, Sent, Archived
- Status transitions are logged with timestamps and reasons
- Partners can view quotation history and status changes
- Expired quotations can be re-created or updated
- Archive functionality maintains historical records

#### FR-010: MUST - Buyer Delivery
**Description**: Approved quotations must be deliverable to buyers with shop branding
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Quotations are shared via secure links to buyers
- Shop branding is prominently displayed
- Partner attribution is maintained in background
- Quotations are mobile-responsive and accessible
- Delivery tracking confirms buyer access

### 7.4 Buyer Experience

#### FR-011: MUST - Quotation Viewing
**Description**: Buyers must be able to view and compare quotations in buyer-friendly interface
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Quotations display shop information and branding
- Product details and specifications are clearly shown
- Pricing and terms are transparent and understandable
- Comparison features help evaluate options
- Contact information is available for questions

#### FR-012: MUST - Dual Payment Modes
**Description**: Buyers must be able to pay via platform checkout or direct payment to shops
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Platform payment option uses Allkons checkout process
- Direct payment option provides shop payment instructions
- Payment method selection is clear and reversible
- Payment processing fees are disclosed upfront
- Receipt generation is supported for both modes

#### FR-013: MUST - Payment Slip Verification
**Description**: Direct payments must support slip upload and verification by shops
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Buyers can upload payment slips for direct payments
- Shops receive notifications for slip verification
- Verification workflow includes approve/reject options
- Verification status updates order progress
- Audit trail maintains slip verification history

#### FR-014: MUST - Order Tracking
**Description**: Buyers must be able to track orders created from quotations
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Order status is displayed in real-time
- Tracking information is updated throughout delivery
- Delivery confirmation triggers commission calculation
- Order history is accessible for reference
- Notifications are sent for status changes

### 7.5 Team & Performance Management

#### FR-015: MUST - Team Structure Visibility
**Description**: Team members must be able to see their team membership and lead identity
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Team members can view their team lead information
- Team structure is clearly displayed
- Contact information for team leads is available
- Team membership changes are communicated
- Team hierarchy is maintained and accurate

#### FR-016: MUST - Team Lead Dashboard
**Description**: Team leads must have access to team performance metrics and management tools
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Team leads can view team member performance metrics
- Sales data is aggregated and trended over time
- Coaching actions can be assigned and tracked
- Team member activity is monitored for compliance
- Performance reports are exportable

#### FR-017: SHOULD - Commission Tracking
**Description**: Partners must be able to track commission earnings and payment status
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Commission dashboard shows earned and pending amounts
- Commission calculation rules are transparent
- Payment history and status are displayed
- Commission attribution is clearly shown
- Dispute resolution process is accessible

### 7.6 Consent & Compliance

#### FR-018: MUST - Consent Management
**Description**: System must integrate with third-party consent center for PDPA compliance
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Consent center integration is established and functional
- Consent records are stored with audit trails
- Consent versioning is supported
- Re-consent workflows are triggered when needed
- Consent withdrawal is processed and honored

#### FR-019: MUST - Data Sharing Consent
**Description**: Buyer data sharing with partners must require explicit consent
**Acceptance Criteria**:
- Data sharing consent is obtained before information exchange
- Consent scope is clearly defined and limited
- Partners can only access consented buyer information
- Consent status is validated before data access
- Audit logs maintain consent and access records

## 10. Risk Assessment & Mitigation

### 10.1 Business Risks

#### Partner Quality Risk
**Risk**: Low-quality partners may damage platform reputation
**Mitigation**:
- Rigorous vetting and approval process
- Performance monitoring and quality metrics
- Clear partner guidelines and compliance requirements
- Regular performance reviews and feedback

#### Shop Adoption Risk
**Risk**: Shops may be reluctant to participate in affiliate program
**Mitigation**:
- Clear value proposition and revenue sharing
- Controlled approval process to maintain quality
- Brand protection and shop identity preservation
- Performance analytics and ROI demonstration

#### Commission Disputes Risk
**Risk**: Disputes over commission calculation and attribution
**Mitigation**:
- Transparent commission calculation rules
- Clear attribution tracking and reporting
- Dispute resolution process and escalation
- Audit trails and documentation

### 10.2 Technical Risks

#### Integration Complexity Risk
**Risk**: Complex integrations may cause system instability
**Mitigation**:
- Phased integration approach with thorough testing
- API versioning and backward compatibility
- Circuit breakers and error handling
- Comprehensive monitoring and alerting

#### Performance Scalability Risk
**Risk**: System may not scale to support partner growth
**Mitigation**:
- Load testing and performance optimization
- Horizontal scaling architecture
- Database optimization and indexing
- CDN and caching strategies

#### Data Security Risk
**Risk**: Data breaches may compromise partner and buyer information
**Mitigation**:
- Encryption and access controls
- Regular security audits and penetration testing
- Data minimization and retention policies
- Incident response and breach notification procedures

### 10.3 Compliance Risks

#### PDPA Compliance Risk
**Risk**: Non-compliance with data protection regulations
**Mitigation**:
- Consent management system integration
- Privacy by design principles
- Regular compliance audits
- Legal review and guidance

#### Financial Regulations Risk
**Risk**: Non-compliance with financial transaction regulations
**Mitigation**:
- Payment processor compliance
- Transaction monitoring and reporting
- Anti-money laundering procedures
- Regulatory guidance and legal review
